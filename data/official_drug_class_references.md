## 1. Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs)

**Source**: NCBI StatPearls NBK539848  
**URL**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK539848/  
**PMID**: 30844151  
**Authors**: Sub Laban T, Saadabadi A  
**Citation**: Laban TS, Saadabadi A. Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOI). StatPearls [Internet]. 2025.

### FDA-Approved MAOIs

#### Nonselective, Irreversible MAOIs (First-Generation)
- **Phenelzine** (Nardil)
- **Tranylcypromine** (Parnate)  
- **Isocarboxazid** (Marplan)

#### Selective MAO-B Inhibitors
- **Selegiline** (oral: Eldepryl, Zelapar; transdermal: Emsam)
- **Rasagiline** (Azilect)
- **Safinamide** (Xadago)

### Critical Drug Interactions (Serotonin Syndrome Risk)
MAOIs have potentially fatal interactions with:
- SSRIs (e.g., fluoxetine, sertraline, paroxetine, citalopram)
- SNRIs (e.g., venlafaxine, duloxetine)
- Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
- Tramadol
- Meperidine
- Dextromethorphan
- Methadone
- St. John's Wort

### Washout Periods
- 2 weeks required when switching from MAOI to another serotonergic drug
- 5 weeks required after stopping fluoxetine before starting MAOI (due to long half-life)

## 3. QT-Prolonging Drugs

**Source**: NCBI StatPearls NBK534864  
**URL**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK534864/  
**PMID**: 30521285  
**Authors**: Farzam K, Tivakaran VS  
**Citation**: Farzam K, Tivakaran VS. QT Prolonging Drugs. StatPearls [Internet]. 2023.

### Antipsychotics
- **Haloperidol** (Haldol)
- **Ziprasidone** (Geodon)
- **Quetiapine** (Seroquel)
- **Thioridazine** (Mellaril)
- **Olanzapine** (Zyprexa)
- **Risperidone** (Risperdal)
- **Droperidol** (Inapsine)

### Antiarrhythmics (Class IA and III)
- **Amiodarone** (Pacerone, Cordarone)
- **Sotalol** (Betapace)
- **Dofetilide** (Tikosyn)
- **Procainamide** (Pronestyl)
- **Quinidine**
- **Flecainide** (Tambocor)

### Antibiotics
**Macrolides:**
- Erythromycin
- Clarithromycin
- Azithromycin

**Fluoroquinolones:**
- Moxifloxacin
- Levofloxacin
- Ciprofloxacin

### Antidepressants
- **Amitriptyline** (Elavil)
- **Imipramine** (Tofranil)
- **Citalopram** (Celexa)
- **Escitalopram** (Lexapro)

### Other QT-Prolonging Agents
- **Methadone** (Dolophine)
- **Sumatriptan** (Imitrex)
- **Ondansetron** (Zofran)
- **Cisapride** (withdrawn from US market)
- **Domperidone** (not approved in US)

### Mechanism
QT prolongation occurs via blockade of hERG (human ether-à-go-go-related gene) potassium channels, delaying cardiac repolarization (Phase 3).

### Clinical Significance
- Normal QTc: ≤440 ms (males), ≤460 ms (females)
- Risk of Torsades de Pointes increases significantly when QTc >500 ms
- Risk factors: hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, hypocalcemia, female sex, bradycardia

## 4. Antiplatelet Agents

**Note**: While not from a single StatPearls article, antiplatelet agents are commonly classified as high-risk for bleeding interactions.

### P2Y12 Receptor Inhibitors
- **Clopidogrel** (Plavix)
- **Prasugrel** (Effient)
- **Ticagrelor** (Brilinta)
- **Cangrelor** (Kengreal) - IV

### Cyclooxygenase (COX) Inhibitors
- **Aspirin** (low-dose for antiplatelet effect)

### Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa Inhibitors
- **Abciximab** (ReoPro)
- **Eptifibatide** (Integrilin)
- **Tirofiban** (Aggrastat)

### Phosphodiesterase Inhibitors
- **Cilostazol** (Pletal)
- **Dipyridamole** (Persantine)

## 5. NSAIDs (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs)

**Clinical Relevance**: NSAIDs increase bleeding risk when combined with anticoagulants/antiplatelets and may interact with cardiovascular medications.

### Non-Selective COX Inhibitors
- Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
- Naproxen (Aleve, Naprosyn)
- Diclofenac (Voltaren)
- Indomethacin (Indocin)
- Piroxicam (Feldene)
- Ketorolac (Toradol)

### COX-2 Selective Inhibitors
- Celecoxib (Celebrex)

## Summary Table for DDI Classification

| Drug Class | Primary Risk | Key Interactions |
|------------|--------------|------------------|
| MAOIs | Serotonin syndrome, hypertensive crisis | SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, tramadol, meperidine, tyramine-rich foods |
| Anticoagulants | Bleeding | Other anticoagulants, antiplatelets, NSAIDs |
| QT-prolonging agents | Torsades de Pointes | Other QT-prolonging drugs, hypokalemia-inducing drugs |
| Antiplatelets | Bleeding | Anticoagulants, other antiplatelets, NSAIDs |
| NSAIDs | GI bleeding, CV events | Anticoagulants, antiplatelets, ACE inhibitors |

---

## Usage in DDI Severity Classification

When classifying DDI severity, interactions involving these drug classes should be given heightened consideration:

1. **Contraindicated**: MAOIs + serotonergic drugs (serotonin syndrome risk)
2. **Major**: Anticoagulant + anticoagulant combinations; QT-prolonging drug combinations
3. **Major**: Anticoagulant + antiplatelet combinations (bleeding risk)
4. **Moderate-Major**: Anticoagulant + NSAID combinations
